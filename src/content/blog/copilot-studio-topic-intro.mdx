If you landed here, you found the corner of the blog dedicated to
**Copilot Studio** — Microsoft's low-code platform for building custom agents
and copilots.

Consider this the welcome mat. There aren't a pile of deep-dive posts here yet,
but this is where they'll live as I publish them. I wanted to put a stake in the
ground so the topic hub has a home and you know what to expect.

## What this topic will cover

Copilot Studio sits at an interesting intersection: it's approachable enough for
makers, but powerful enough to build agents that plug into real business
systems. The posts I'm planning here will walk that whole range, including:

- **Building agents from scratch** — topics, triggers, and conversation design
  that actually holds up with real users.
- **Grounding on your data** — connecting knowledge sources so answers are
  accurate instead of confidently wrong.
- **Actions and connectors** — letting agents *do* things, not just talk, by
  wiring up Power Platform connectors and custom actions.
- **Testing and publishing** — moving from a demo that works on your screen to
  something you can hand to the business.
- **Governance and rollout** — the guardrails, environments, and DLP policies
  that keep an agent program from becoming a shadow-IT problem.

## Why a separate topic

Copilot Studio used to sit alongside Microsoft 365 Copilot on this blog, but the
two are really different jobs. M365 Copilot is about *extending and adopting* the
assistant Microsoft ships. Copilot Studio is about *building your own*. They
deserve their own spaces, so here we are.
